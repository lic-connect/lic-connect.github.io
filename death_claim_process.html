Death Claim Process

Inform LIC About the Death

As soon as possible, the nominee or legal heir must inform the nearest LIC branch office about the death. This can be done in person or by calling customer service.

Collect Necessary Documents

  • Death Certificate: From the municipal authority.
  • Original Policy Document: The original policy bond.
  • Claimant's Statement (Form 3783): The main death claim form.
  • NEFT Mandate Form: For direct bank transfer, with a cancelled cheque or passbook copy.
  • Proof of Identity & Address: Claimant's Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, etc.
  • Proof of Age of Deceased: If not previously verified.
  • Medical/Hospital/Employer Certificates (Form 3815/3816): Depending on the cause of death (illness or accident).
  • Police FIR & Post-Mortem Report: Required for accidental or unnatural deaths.

Fill Out the Death Claim Form (No. 3783)

The nominee must fill in personal details, policy number, cause and date of death. Attach all required documents and sign the form. Attestation by a competent authority may be required.

Visit the Nearest LIC Branch

Submit all documents at the policy's servicing branch or any nearby branch. An official will verify the documents and provide an acknowledgment receipt.

Investigation (If Required)

LIC may conduct a thorough investigation if the death occurs within three years of policy issuance or if the cause is suspicious. This may extend the processing time.

Receive the Death Claim Amount

The claim amount will be credited directly to the claimant's bank account via NEFT. LIC will send a letter confirming the settlement.

Tax Implications

Typically, the death benefit received is tax-free under Section 10(10D) of the Income Tax Act. It is always advisable to consult a tax advisor for clarity.
